Water Seller

This work is from my project Sakka, which explores the figure of traditional water sellers in Egypt and my personal connection to this cultural history.

The work is based on the story of a woman who, after the death of her husband, continued his role as a water seller until her own passing. This painting speaks to continuity, labour, and devotion, while also challenging traditional expectations of gender within this role.

Through layered materials and textured surfaces, the painting reflects both the physicality of the work and the passage of time, suggesting a life shaped by persistence and quiet strength.
