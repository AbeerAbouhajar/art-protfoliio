Charcoal and Oil on MDF Board2024

A Gathering

This piece emerges from my engagement with the personal archive that I have created of my uncle, the late artist Deep Almasri, where memory appears as incomplete and shifting rather than fixed.

Working from fragmented visual references, the image resists clarity and resolution. The charcoal marks reflect the instability of recollection, where forms dissolve and reappear, suggesting that what is remembered is continuously reconstructed rather than retrieved.

The sketch holds space for uncertainty, absence, and interpretation.
